BIOGRAPHY




Born in Congo, Brazzaville in the year of 2000, Heston Ngouesse grew up with her grandmother while her mother pursued an education in the U.S. At the age of eight, she traveled to the United States with her cousin and grandmother to finally meet with her new family.
"I had left my old life behind as the plane flew its way into the sky and I begun my new life as the speck of my feet touched the American grounds. I breathed a different air and my ear was fed by a different Tongue. Just like Nina Simone once said in her song- "it's a new dawn, it's a new day, it's a new life for me" and yes indeed it was." "The most difficult challenge I had to overcome was not learning a new language as it is for most foreigners. Mine was simply fitting in...it was finding a way to be excepted the way I was. And that was the hardest."

Heston Ngouesse is the eldest of 4 siblings, three brothers, and one sister. Her school life was well balanced as well as her social life. She prioritized getting good grades throughout high school, as well as taking Ap classes.
"French is my mother tongue! I have soccer skills, music, and art in my blood. I am currently learning the guitar which is harder than the piano actually."
Heston is most passionate about art and the ability to express herself through it. Whether it is through music/ singing, drawing, or taking a good. "It all counts either way!"
"I think that making myself feel happy should be top priority! Accomplishing my dream for myself first. Also becoming someone important in this world is something that's always been in my mind and that's where my ambitions stand."
